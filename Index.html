The Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM), also known as Mangalyaan, marked a major milestone for India’s space program. Launched in 2013 by ISRO, it was India’s first interplanetary mission and reached Mars orbit on September 24, 2014. The mission was praised for its cost-effectiveness, having been completed for just $74 million. It provided critical data on Mars’ atmosphere, morphology, and surface composition for over eight years before contact was lost in 2022. Mangalyaan demonstrated India’s ability to execute complex space missions and inspired global recognition for its achievements.
